# ADR 002: Multi-Step Phone Normalization Strategy
## Status

Accepted

## Decision

Normalize phone numbers through a multi-step transformation process before performing fraud analysis.

## Context

Customer phone numbers arrive in multiple formats:

- Different punctuation
- Country codes
- Area-code variations
- User-entered formatting
- Invalid values

Without normalization, duplicate detection becomes unreliable.

## Decision Rationale

The normalization process follows three logical phases:

1. Remove formatting characters
2. Standardize the numeric representation
3. Validate the resulting value

This approach improves consistency while keeping the logic easy to understand and maintain.

## Consequences

### Positive

- Improved duplicate detection
- More reliable customer matching
- Reduced false positives

### Negative

- Some uncommon formats may be discarded
- Requires periodic review as data evolves

## Alternatives Considered

### Single Complex Regular Expression

Rejected because it becomes difficult to maintain.

### User Defined Functions (UDFs)

Rejected because SQL-based transformations are easier to audit and optimize.

## Outcome

Phone normalization became a foundational component for several fraud indicators.
