# ADR 006: Shared Contact Detection

## Status

Accepted

## Decision

Use shared contact information as a fraud indicator.

## Context

Multiple customer accounts occasionally reuse:

- Email addresses
- Phone numbers

While not inherently fraudulent, this behavior may indicate elevated risk depending on surrounding signals.

## Decision Rationale

The system evaluates:

- Shared email addresses
- Shared phone numbers

after normalization and validation.

The objective is to identify relationships between accounts that may otherwise appear independent.

## Consequences

### Positive

- Strong behavioral signal
- Improves account-linkage analysis
- Supports manual investigations

### Negative

- Legitimate shared contacts may exist
- Must be combined with other indicators

## Alternatives Considered

### Ignore Shared Contact Information

Rejected because valuable fraud patterns would be missed.

## Outcome

Shared-contact detection became one of the core fraud-analysis components.
